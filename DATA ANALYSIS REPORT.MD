## Introduction

Bank of America consumer complaints - Consumer satisfaction dashboard

## Project Discription

This project analyses a synthetic dataset modelled after Bank of America Consumer Complaints from **2017-2023**, it focuses on understanding customers dissatisfaction, complaints pattern and service performance, in order to improve response process, reduce complaints and strengthen customers trust.


## Project Aim

The aim of this project is to understand why customers compalints are on the high, by carrying out a professional analysis of the dataset, and come up with actionable insights upon which recommendations will be made to solve the problem of customers dissatisfaction and complaints the bank is currently facing. In view of this a 2 page dashboard was created, using Power BI, which focuses on Customer compplaints overview and distribution, with which actionable insights will be provided for stakeholders use.

---

## Business Questions

The following business questions guided our analysis:

- From which of the states is the bank receiving the most customers dissatisfaction and complaints?
- Which products and services are the ones generating the most customers complaints?
- What does the trend of complaints looks like over the period in question? Is it increaing or decreasing?
- Whta is the time lag as regards when complaints were received, when response was processed and when it was finally closed?

## Key Insights

1. Customer Complaints Are Highly Concentrated in a Few States: California recorded the highest number of complaints (13,709), accounting for a significant share of all 62,516 complaints. Florida (6,488) and Texas (4,686) follow behind, indicating that customer issues are concentrated in a few key states

2. : Checking & Savings Accounts Generate the Most Complaints: The Checking or Savings Account product received 24,814 complaints, followed by Credit Cards (16,197) and Credit Reporting (7,710). Together, these three product categories account for the majority of customer complaints, suggesting they should be the primary focus for product enhancement and service quality improvements.

3. Strong Response Performance, but Root Causes Persist: The organization achieved a 93.77% Timely Response Rate, demonstrating excellent responsiveness to customer complaints. However, the large number of complaints related to Managing an Account and Incorrect Information suggests that while issues are being addressed promptly, recurring operational problems remain. 

4. Although the organization demonstrates strong overall responsiveness, reducing late and pending responses will further improve customer satisfaction and regulatory compliance.

5. The downward trend suggests improvements in service quality or successful complaint prevention initiatives over time

## Recommendations

1. The management should find out what those states with very low numbers of complaints were doing well that those with high numbers of complaints were not doing well, and use that template to organize training for members of staff of the states where most or high numbers of complaints were received. This for sure will reduce the number of complaints receive from customers of the bank from these states and the bank as a whole drastically. 

**Stakeholders**: The regional and branch managers

2. The management should review and redesign the operational process of those products that generated the highest numbers of complaints from the consumers, especially the Checking or Savings Account that generated the highest number, with a total of 24,814, which is like 1/3 of the total complaints received from all products. This can be done by simplifying the operational process more for the customers of these products. This will remove a lot of bottlenecks that make it difficult to access and operate these accounts and as such reduce the number of complaints generated by these products.

**Stakeholders**: Products Managers

3. A system that will focus on preventive measures and process improvements should be created, as this will have a greater long-term impact on reducing the causes of the complaints rather than responding quickly alone. A root cause analysis should also be carried out to identify the causes and put in place measures that will ensure that this issue is no longer a recurring decimal. A refresher training should also be organized for the Account Officers in order to update their skills and knowledge as regards the modern efficient and effective methods of managing customer’s account. This will ensure that the same mistake does not keep occurring.

**Stakeholders**: Products and Account Managers

4. Investigation should be carried out to know the causes of untimely responses and implement service-level agreement (SLA) monitoring. Priority should also be given to resolving the backlog of 1,494 pending complaints. In order to fast track the clearing of these pending complaints, adhoc staff can be employed to achieve this.

**Stakeholders**: Customer Care Manager

5. Analyze the factors behind the quarterly decline in complaints and sustain those initiatives.

**Stakeholders** Products Managers
